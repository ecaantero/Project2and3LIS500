Our Tech Heroes

Moses West

Our first tech hero is Moses West, the founder of AWG Contracting. He is the engineer behind a solar powered water generator that provides clean drinking water in disaster stricken and underserved communities. His technology continued to supply nearly 400 gallons of water daily in Accompong, Jamaica, even after Hurricane Melissa destroyed local infrastructure. West focuses on delivering safe, renewable, off-grid water solutions to communities affected by water insecurity, including Flint, Jackson, and Puerto Rico. He is now scaling production in the U.S. through a $25 million clean-manufacturing initiative to expand global access to climate-resilient water technology.

Kande Sill

Out second tech hero is Kande Sill. After learning about her, I was amazed that at just 16 years old she was awarded the prestigious Arkwright Engineering Scholarship. She earned this recognition because her work is both highly relevant and impactful, especially in supporting underserved communities. What inspires me most is how she proves that age is just a number. Through her project, “Enhancing Medical Delivery,” she explores how drones can be used to securely transport critical medical supplies, demonstrating real-world problem solving.
